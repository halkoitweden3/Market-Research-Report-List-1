<p><p>Weakly Basic Anion Exchange Resins are classified into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I resins have a higher capacity for hydroxide ions and are commonly used in applications requiring higher pH stability, such as water treatment. Type II resins, on the other hand, possess a lower degree of basicity and are effective in removing specific anions at lower pH levels. Both types are essential in various industries, including pharmaceuticals, food and beverage, and environmental applications.</p></p>
